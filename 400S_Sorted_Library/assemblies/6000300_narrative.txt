3. Structural Relationships
3.1 Mate Architecture
The 82 mates are distributed as: 52 Coincident (surface alignment and positioning), 27 Concentric (shaft/bore axis alignment), 2 Parallel (track orientation), and 1 Distance (shaft axial positioning). Coincident mates dominate because the sheetmetal body, chassis, and enclosure panels rely on face-to-face contact for location. Concentric mates are concentrated in the drive train where shafts pass through bores in the chassis, wheels, and axle tubes.
3.2 Primary Structure
The lower chassis (lime, 1030009) serves as the primary structural datum. It accepts the shafts concentrically (3 concentric mates), seats the body via coincident mates (2 mates to main body), anchors the blade pivot (1 concentric), and positions the tracks (2 coincident). Nearly every other component references the chassis directly or indirectly. The main sheetmetal body (pink, 1030010) forms the secondary structure, with mates to the grill (2), floor and bench (2), back plate (2), lower chassis (2), and motor brace (1).
